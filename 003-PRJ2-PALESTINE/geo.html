Geography and Borders of the Territories

Geographical Overview

The region encompasses a diverse landscape, including coastal plains, mountains, and desert areas. Key geographical features include the Mediterranean coastline, the Jordan River, and the Negev Desert.

Major Cities

Significant urban centers include Jerusalem, Tel Aviv, and Hebron. Each city holds historical, cultural, and religious importance, influencing demographic and political dynamics.

Borders and Territorial Divisions

The modern borders of Israel and the Palestinian territories are complex and contested. Key areas include:

  • West Bank: A landlocked territory bordered by Israel to the west and Jordan to the east, home to a significant Palestinian population.
  • Gaza Strip: A narrow coastal strip bordering Israel and Egypt, governed by Hamas since 2007.
  • Golan Heights: A strategically important area annexed by Israel, bordering Syria.

Environmental Challenges

The region faces environmental issues such as water scarcity, land degradation, and urbanization pressures, affecting both communities and agriculture.

Geopolitical Importance

The territories are strategically located at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa, making them significant in regional and global geopolitics.

Understanding the geography and borders of the region is essential for grasping the historical and contemporary conflicts that shape its narrative.
