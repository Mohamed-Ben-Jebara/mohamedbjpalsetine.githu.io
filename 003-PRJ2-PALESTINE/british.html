The British Mandate in Palestine (1917-1947)

British Capture of Jerusalem (1917)

During World War I, British forces capture Jerusalem in December 1917, marking the beginning of British administration in Palestine. The Balfour Declaration of 1917 expresses support for the establishment of a "national home for the Jewish people" in Palestine.

League of Nations Mandate (1920)

The League of Nations formally grants Britain the mandate over Palestine in 1920. The mandate aims to implement the Balfour Declaration while ensuring the rights of the existing Arab population.

Jewish Immigration and Tensions

The 1920s and 1930s see increased Jewish immigration, fueled by rising anti-Semitism in Europe and Zionist aspirations. This leads to growing tensions between Jewish and Arab communities, resulting in violent clashes.

Arab Revolt (1936-1939)

The Arab Revolt occurs in response to British policies and Jewish immigration. The revolt is met with a heavy-handed military response from the British, leading to significant unrest and repression.
