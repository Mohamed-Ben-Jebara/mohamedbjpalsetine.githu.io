Ancient Period

Ancient Period:

Neolithic period (-8300 to -4800):

The first human settlements in Palestine were found in the Jordan Valley, with evidence of early migrations of Homo erectus out of Africa.

Chalcolithic period (-4300 to -3300):

The Ghassulian culture developed in Palestine, with the use of copper and the establishment of small settlements.

Bronze Age (-3300 to -1200):

The Canaanite civilization developed in Palestine, with the establishment of cities and trade with Egypt.

The Bronze Age is divided into three periods:

Early Bronze Age (-3300 to -2200):

The Canaanite cities were fortified, and trade with Egypt increased.

Middle Bronze Age (-2200 to -1550):

The Canaanite civilization reached its peak, with the establishment of city-states and trade with Egypt.

Late Bronze Age (-1550 to -1200):

The Canaanite civilization declined, with the rise of the Hyksos in Egypt and the eventual conquest of Palestine by the Egyptians.

Iron Age and the First Ethnic States

Iron Age I (-1200 to -980)

The first Israelites settle in mountainous areas, which are less exposed to invasions. This culture emerges in continuity with the previous Canaanite culture.

Iron Age II (-980 to -700)

Sheshonq I attacks the kingdom, while Hazael ends Omride dominance. Assyria invades Israel in -720.

Iron Age III (-732 to -586)

In -586, Nebuchadnezzar conquers Judah, destroys the Temple, and deports the population, marking the beginning of the Jewish diaspora.
