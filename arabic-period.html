arabic period:

Islamic Conquest (638):

Following the death of Muhammad, Muslim armies rapidly expand, capturing Jerusalem from the Byzantines. The city becomes an important center for Islam.

Umayyad and Abbasid Periods:

Umayyad Caliphate (661-750):

Significant architectural developments, including the Dome of the Rock (691). The region flourishes economically and culturally.

Focus shifts to Baghdad, but Palestine remains vital for trade and pilgrimage.

Fatimid Period (909-1171):

The Fatimids, a Shia dynasty, rule over Palestine, promoting religious tolerance and economic prosperity. Jerusalem sees a resurgence in population and importance.

Crusades and Decline of Muslim Control (1096):

The First Crusade begins in 1096, leading to the capture of Jerusalem by Crusaders in 1099. This marks the end of Muslim dominance in the city for nearly a century.
